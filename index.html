HiSTalk

Hierarchical Speech Feature-based Landmark Displacements for 3D Talking Head Animation

Paper Demo Code

Demos and comparisons with CodeTalker and FaceFormer.

Abstract

Speech-driven 3D facial animation has gained significant attention for its ability to create realistic and expressive facial animations in 3D space based on speech. Landmarks efficiently describe speech-related movements, but existing methods often use only frame-level features, overlooking multi-level temporal contexts (phoneme, word, utterance), leading to over-smoothed animations. We propose HiSTalk, a novel framework that correlates hierarchical speech features with landmark displacements. Our method comprises two modules: HSF2S (Hierarchical Speech Features to Sparse landmarks) and S2D (Sparse landmarks to Dense landmarks). HSF2S encodes multi-level cues via a squeeze-and-excitation gating mechanism, while S2D uses a multi-branch Transformer decoder to generate dense deformation fields, ensuring accurate lip-sync and expressive motion. Extensive experiments demonstrate state-of-the-art performance.

Proposed Method

Our pipeline extracts hierarchical speech embeddings (frame, phoneme, word, utterance) using Wav2Vec 2.0 and SpeechFormer++ blocks. The HSF2S encoder computes weighted sparse landmark offsets. The S2D decoder fuses these offsets via multi-branch attention to produce dense 3D facial deformations.

HSF2S Encoder Overview

The encoder aligns different-level features to landmark displacements with a squeeze-and-excitation weight generator.
